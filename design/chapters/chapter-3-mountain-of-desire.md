## Narrative intro

The monk climbs a mountain where treasure hunters and merchants are obsessed with gold and power. A **Golden Titan** guards the peak, born from the collective greed of those who sought “more.” The monk must navigate traps, temptations (e.g. optional treasure vs. risk), and greedy enemies, then face the Titan to learn that gratitude—not accumulation—brings peace.

---

## Emotional theme

- **Greed** as the central struggle: never enough, comparison, and the cost of “endless hunger.”
- The Titan is the embodiment of insatiable desire; defeating it is about shifting from “wanting more” to “enough.”
- Resolution is framed as **gratitude and contentment**, not hoarding.

---

## Gameplay objectives

1. **Explore the mountain** — Paths with optional treasure, hazards, and NPCs obsessed with wealth.
2. **Side objectives** — Resist or redirect greed (e.g. choose not to take everything; help someone let go).
3. **Main objective** — Overcome **Greed-spawned enemies** (e.g. possessive spirits, gold-armored brutes) and environmental traps.
4. **Boss objective** — Defeat the **Golden Titan** at the peak.

Objective types: *Kill X enemies*, *Defeat boss*, *Collect fragments* (with a “enough, not everything” twist), or *Survive waves*.

## Boss: Golden Titan

| Attribute | Notes |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Golden Titan |
| **Visual** | Massive, gold/amber, imposing; slow but heavy hits. |
| **Behavior** | Punishes greed: e.g. dropping “treasure” that damages or debuffs if the player prioritizes it over safety. Rewards patience and choosing “enough.” |
| **Mechanics** | Phase shifts or mechanics that tempt the player (e.g. loot orbs vs. safe positioning); resisting greed weakens the Titan or opens damage windows. |
| **Reward** | 150 XP (boss), plus quest completion. |

Design should reinforce: “Gratitude ends endless hunger”—knowing when to stop and be grateful is key.
